A Brick's Dream

observations

have to admit , I neither have seen the movie nor do I know any context of this scene, but from the comments, i do infer the movie may be kinda sus. 👀😷


Now I pick it up from there,
Even a brick dreams of becoming something greater than itself.
It dreams of  being a part of something greater, to transcend its material reality, to achieve an eternal presence.

For a brick to realize such a feat ,
It strives to be part of an Architectural Excellence , maybe a Renaissance Gothic Cathedral , a  Chola Temple, or an Ottoman Mosque.
It's the will to be a word in the Great Narration. To leave a lasting relevance,
that drives the Bricks' existence.

Now, in the case of most bricks that came into this existence, most of them never achieve their dream and deteriorate into scattered particles of sand.

Before its baking, a brick is nothing more than individual particles of sand. It is this cosmic perpetual combination that puts together to a single unique brick.

While it is, in fact, a magnanimous presence of forces and matter coming together in a special combination to combine a billion sand particles in a particular proportion under a certain energy state to bake a perfect brick. As the Brick Carl Sagan would puts it "We the Bricks are nothing but Stardust..

Yet the brick thinks of itself as an individual entity, an isolated presence.

Nevertheless, the energy eventually does fall apart into bits and pieces. particles of sand.
This whole universe is one bhrammanda dance of particles , crashing into each other to make sweet moments of contact only to drift apart and crash another and another…to ♾️

Here, the brick doesn't get  any say in  choosing its destiny. It never by itself manages to lay itself among the finest of monuments.
It's the act of a higher sentient that decides whether the brick gets to make it in Qutub Minar or to be used as footsteps in a puddle.

The only thing a brick can control (to a certain extent) is , to hold itself together in its Cuboid perfection or let itself crumble into near infinite pieces by Daily friction.

But no matter how many times a brick gets disintegrated into dust. All the dust eventually does end up resurrected as bricks.
